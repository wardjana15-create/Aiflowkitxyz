Why This Checklist?

Most people fail at automation because they start too big.

This guide walks you through automating your first task — start to finish — in under 30 minutes.

No code. No email. No paywall. Just copy these steps.

Step 1: Audit Your Repetitive Tasks

Open a blank document. Write down 3 tasks you do weekly that feel robotic. Examples:

  • Save Gmail attachments to Google Drive
  • Post new blog links to Twitter + LinkedIn
  • Turn meeting notes into summary emails

Pick ONE to start. We’ll automate: “Save Gmail attachments to Google Drive.”

Step 2: Sign Up for Make (Free Tier)

Go to make.com → Click “Sign Up” → Use Google or email.

Choose “Free plan” — it allows 1,000 operations/month — enough for this tutorial.

Verify your email → Log in.

Step 3: Create Your First Scenario

In Make dashboard, click “Create a new scenario” → Name it: “Gmail → Google Drive”.

Click “Add your first module” → Search for “Gmail” → Select “Watch Emails”.

Click “Add” → Click “Create a connection” → Log in to your Gmail → Allow permissions.

Step 4: Configure Gmail Trigger

In “Watch Emails” module:

  • Connection: Your Gmail account
  • Label: Leave blank (or create label “ToSave” if preferred)
  • Limit: 1 (process one email at a time)

Click “OK” → Click “Run once” to test → Check green checkmark.

Step 5: Add Google Drive Module

Click “+” after Gmail module → Search for “Google Drive” → Select “Upload a File”.

Click “Add” → “Create a connection” → Log in to Google Drive → Allow permissions.

Step 6: Configure File Upload

In “Upload a File” module:

  • Connection: Your Google Drive
  • File name: {{2.filename}} (click mapping icon → select “Attachments” → “Filename”)
  • File content: {{2.content}} (click mapping → “Attachments” → “Content”)
  • Folder: Click folder icon → Choose or create folder “Saved Attachments”

Click “OK”.

Step 7: Activate & Test

Click “Turn scenario ON” (top right) → Set schedule: “Every 15 minutes”.

Send yourself a test email with an attachment.

Wait 15 minutes → Check your Google Drive → File should appear in “Saved Attachments”.

✅ Done. You just automated your first task.
